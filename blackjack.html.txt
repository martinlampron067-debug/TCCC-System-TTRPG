        <h2>The Lite Philosophy</h2>
        <p>TCCC Lite is a streamlined adaptation of the parent engine found in <em>Saeculum</em>. It is designed to be a "fast-filler"—perfect for one-shots or sessions where you want tactical depth without the weight of a 300-page manual.</p>
        
        <div class="tech-box">
            <h3>Blackjack Resolution</h3>
            <p>Unlike standard d20 systems, the Lite engine often utilizes a "push your luck" style resolution. This creates immediate tension: do you play it safe or risk a "bust" for a critical cinematic success?</p>
        </div>
    </section>

    <section style="margin-top: 50px;">
        <h2>Trouble at the Black Jack Saloon</h2>
        <p>The premier module for the TCCC Lite system, transporting players to the unforgiving grit of the Old West.</p>
        
        <div class="tech-box">
            <h3>No "HP Bloat"</h3>
            <p>In this system, you don't have a massive pool of health. Every shot counts. Wounds are tracked specifically, and a single well-placed bullet can permanently alter your character's effectiveness for the rest of the encounter.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="tech-box">
            <h3>Veteran-Informed Mechanics</h3>
            <p>Drawing from the author's 18 years of service in the Canadian Armed Forces, the combat mechanics focus on <span class="highlight">Tactical Leverage</span>. Positioning, cover, and the weight of your choices matter more than just the numbers on your sheet.</p>
        </div>
    </section>

    <section style="margin-top: 50px;">
        <h2>Key Features</h2>
        <ul>
            <li><span class="highlight">Bilingual Support:</span> Fully playable in both English and Français.</li>
            <li><span class="highlight">Zero-Prep:</span> Designed to get you from character creation to the first shootout in minutes.</li>
            <li><span class="highlight">Cinematic Lethality:</span> Encourages creative solutions to avoid the deadly consequences of direct conflict.</li>
